# How to Play Blackjack

This document explains the rules and gameplay flow used in this Blackjack game. The game follows standard casino rules, including splitting, doubling, surrendering, and insurance.

## Overview

Blackjack is played against the dealer. Your goal is to finish with a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer’s hand, without going over (going bust).

### Card Values

- Number cards = their number
- Face cards = 10
- Aces = 1 (soft) or 11 (automatically chosen to your advantage)

### Start of Round

- You start the game with 100 units of 'cash' in your stack.
- Place your bet by dragging chips onto the golden ring betting area.
- You and the dealer each receive two cards.
- One of the dealer’s cards is face‑up, and both of your cards are face-up

### Player Action

After the initial deal, choose how to play your hand.  The game will let you know what options are available yo you at every stage of play.

#### Hit

- Draw another card.
- If the total value of your hand exceeds 21, you go bust and lose your bet on that hand

#### Stand

- Don't draw any more cards, maintaining your current total hand value.
- You will automatically stand if you reach 21.

#### Double

- Double your bet, draw one additional card, and end your turn
- Only available on your initial two card hand

#### Split

- If the two cards in your initial hand have the same value, you may split them into two separate hands.
- Each hand draws a new second card
- You then play each hand independently
- This game allows unlimited resplits. So if you keep drawing the same value cards, you can keep splitting (as long as you can afford it)

#### Surrender

- End your turn immediately and lose half your bet.
- Only available on your initial two card hand

#### Insurance

- If the dealer’s face‑up card is an Ace, you will be offered insurance (as long as you can afford it)
- This is a side bet of half of your original bet.
- If you accept this offer, and the dealer has blackjack, you win this insurance bet.
- If the dealer doesn't have blackjack, you lose this insurance bet.

### Dealer Action

After you finish your turn, the dealer plays:

- The dealer reveals their face-down card
- The dealer will continue to draw until their total hand value is 17 or higher (including soft-17)

### End of Round

Once the dealer finishes their turn, results are calculated:

- Each of your hands is compared to the dealer’s hand
- You win if your total is higher than the dealer’s without busting
- You lose if you go bust or the dealer has a higher total
- A tie results in a 'push' and your bet is returned
- If you run out of money, the game ends, with an option to start again.
